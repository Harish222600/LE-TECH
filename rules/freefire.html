Basic rules about this event:

Here's a one-line description, rules, and regulations for esports:
One-line Description:
Esports is a competitive gaming platform where individuals or teams compete against each other in various video games, requiring skill, strategy, and teamwork.
General Rules:
1. Game Selection: Competitors will play a predetermined game or series of games.
2. Team Size: Competitors will participate in teams of a specified size (e.g., solo, duo, squad).
3. Match Format: Matches will be played in a best-of-series format (e.g., best-of-three, best-of-five).
4. Gameplay: Competitors must play the game in accordance with the game's rules and settings.
5. Conduct: Competitors must conduct themselves in a respectful and sporting manner.
Regulations:
1. Player Eligibility: Competitors must meet specific eligibility requirements (e.g., age, residency).
2. Team Registration: Teams must register prior to the competition and provide required information.
3. Equipment: Competitors must use approved equipment (e.g., gaming PCs, consoles).
4. Cheating: Any form of cheating, hacking, or exploiting will result in disqualification.
5. Prize Distribution: Prizes will be awarded to the winners in accordance with the competition's prize structure.
Penalties:
1. Disqualification: Competitors who violate the rules or regulations may be disqualified.
2. Penalty Points: Competitors who commit minor infractions may receive penalty points.
3. Forfeiture: Competitors who fail to appear or participate in a match may forfeit the match.
Note: These rules and regulations are general and may vary depending on the specific esports competition or tournament.
